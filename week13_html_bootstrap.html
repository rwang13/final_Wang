JNL221 Final: Child Soldier Abuse Project

Proposed by Franklin Wang

NUT GRAF: The exploitation of child soldiers is immoral. In 2009, the Child Soldiers Prevention Act (CSPA) officially took effect to protect the rights of minors. Annually, the CSPA requests a list from the U.S. Secretary for countries that recruited child soldiers. Once identified, the United States will no longer financially support them unless national orders are given. This data project aims to see if, and how much, money is sent by the U.S. governments each year if countries that request military funds obey the CSPA.

FINDING 1: Nearly 8 billion dollars are waived by the U.S. to support the Child Soldier Protection Act, according to Arms and Assistance Data from 2011 to 2021.
